## Project Overview
This project focuses on the modeling and analysis of a complex system subject to uncertainty. By leveraging **Discrete-Event Simulation (DES)**, the goal was to capture the stochastic nature of the system, identify performance bottlenecks, and propose data-driven improvements without disrupting the actual physical operations.

## Problem Statement
In many real-world systems, variability in arrival times and service rates leads to unpredictable queue lengths and resource underutilization. The primary challenge was to map the actual process flow, understand the underlying probability distributions of the variables, and evaluate the system's performance under various stress scenarios.

## Methodology & Stochastic Modeling
To build an accurate digital twin of the process, the following steps were executed:
* **Input Data Analysis:** Collected historical data and applied statistical goodness-of-fit tests (e.g., Chi-Square, Kolmogorov-Smirnov) to determine the appropriate probability distributions (such as Exponential, Normal, or Weibull) for stochastic variables.
* **Model Development:** Designed the logical flow of the system, including queues, resource constraints, and routing logic.
* **Verification & Validation:** Ensured the simulation model accurately represented the real-world system through steady-state analysis and comparing the simulated Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) with historical outputs.

## Key Results & Bottleneck Analysis
Through extensive **What-If analysis** and running multiple replications, the simulation revealed:
* Precise identification of the system's primary bottlenecks.
* Optimal resource allocation strategies that significantly reduced the average waiting time in queues.
* A robust capacity planning framework for future demand surges.
